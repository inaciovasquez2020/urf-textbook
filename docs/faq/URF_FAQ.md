# Unified Rigidity Framework — FAQ

## What is URF?
The Unified Rigidity Framework studies structural limits of inference
and reconstruction processes under two constraints:
locality of information and finite information capacity.

## What problem does URF address?
Many algorithms attempt to reconstruct global structure from local
information through iterative refinement. URF investigates when this
process necessarily fails due to structural rigidity.

## What are the main mathematical ingredients?
Core components include:
- FO^k local types
- cycle-overlap rank
- entropy-depth bounds
- structural rigidity lemmas

## What is EntropyDepth?
EntropyDepth measures the minimum number of refinement steps required
to eliminate structural ambiguity in a system with bounded information flow.

## What is Cycle–Local Rigidity?
The principle that sufficiently rich cycle overlap forces diversity of
local logical types, preventing homogeneous local structure.

## Why multiple repositories?
The URF ecosystem separates roles:

urf-core
Foundational definitions and axioms.

urf-spine
Canonical structural lemmas.

chronos-urf-rr
Reference experiments and computational analysis.

urf-textbook
Exposition and pedagogical structure.

## Is URF a finished theory?
No. The framework is an active research program with several open
mathematical questions and ongoing validation.

## How can researchers engage with the framework?
Possible directions include:
- proving structural lemmas
- extending rigidity invariants
- conducting computational experiments
- formalizing results in proof assistants.
