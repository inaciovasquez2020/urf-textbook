# Related Work and Positioning

## Purpose
This document positions the Unified Rigidity Framework (URF) relative to
existing research areas and classical results.

## Fields Connected

- Computational Complexity
- Graph Theory
- Mathematical Physics
- Dynamical Systems
- Information Theory

## Conceptual Context

URF studies limits of inference and reconstruction under two constraints:

1. Locality of information
2. Finite information capacity of refinement processes

This places the framework near several classical themes.

## Related Ideas

### Locality and Logic
Local definability and FO^k methods in finite model theory.

### Structural Graph Constraints
Expansion phenomena, cycle structure, and combinatorial rigidity.

### Complexity Barriers
Limits on algorithmic inference and structural obstructions to efficient computation.

### Spectral and Geometric Methods
Connections between structure, rigidity, and spectral properties.

## URF Contribution

URF proposes a unified mechanism:

Locality + finite information capacity ⇒ structural rigidity.

This mechanism provides a structural explanation for limits of refinement,
inference, and reconstruction processes across multiple domains.

## Open Direction

Future work includes deeper connections with:

- expanders and spectral graph theory
- logical definability hierarchies
- dynamical rigidity phenomena
- algorithmic complexity barriers
