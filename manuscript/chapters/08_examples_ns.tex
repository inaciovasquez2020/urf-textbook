\chapter{Example: Navier--Stokes}

\section{Evolution and Dissipation}

Fluid evolution combines local transport, pressure coupling, and dissipation.  A URF-style treatment separates energy control from regularity transfer.

\begin{definition}[Regularity certificate]
A regularity certificate is a finite collection of estimates sufficient to propagate smoothness or exclude blow-up in the encoded evolution class.
\end{definition}

\section{Energy Barrier}

Energy inequalities alone do not automatically imply smoothness.  A complete argument requires a bridge from controlled energy or dissipation into pointwise or higher-regularity bounds.

\begin{lemma}[Conditional regularity transfer]
If a regularity certificate supplies uniform higher-norm control on the relevant interval, then blow-up is excluded on that interval.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Uniform higher-norm control is incompatible with the assumed blow-up criterion.
\end{proof}
