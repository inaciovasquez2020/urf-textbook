\chapter{Example: Riemann Hypothesis}

\section{Spectral Encoding}

Some approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis seek a spectral or operator-theoretic encoding of zeros.  URF treats such approaches as rigidity problems only after the encoding and coercivity claims are explicitly supplied.

\begin{definition}[Spectral certificate interface]
A spectral certificate interface consists of an operator model, a zero-detection map, and a positivity or localization invariant sufficient to transfer spectral rigidity into arithmetic information.
\end{definition}

\section{Missing Bridge}

The central missing bridge is not terminology.  It is the construction of a valid spectral certificate with the required arithmetic soundness.

\begin{remark}
A framework can record the shape of the desired proof, but cannot promote a missing spectral certificate into a theorem.
\end{remark}

\section{Boundary}

This chapter is an explanatory interface only.  It does not prove the Riemann Hypothesis or any equivalent arithmetic statement.
