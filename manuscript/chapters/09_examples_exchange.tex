\chapter{Example: Exchange Force}

\section{Exchange as Constraint}

Exchange effects can be described as constraints imposed by admissible interaction channels, symmetry, and conservation rules.

\begin{definition}[Exchange constraint]
An exchange constraint is a rule limiting which transitions are admissible between two encoded configurations.
\end{definition}

\section{Rigidity Interpretation}

When exchange constraints remove degrees of freedom, the remaining dynamics may become rigid.  The URF role is to name the obstruction and specify what certificate would prove the claimed rigidity.

\begin{example}[Finite exchange gate]
A finite exchange gate allows only a bounded set of admissible transitions.  If the target transition is outside that set, it is forbidden inside the encoded model.
\end{example}
