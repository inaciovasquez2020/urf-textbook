\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}

This book develops the Unified Rigidity Framework, abbreviated URF, as a structural language for finite-capacity refinement, locality, and terminal obstruction.

The guiding principle is that global behavior is constrained by local information capacity.  The framework is not introduced as a solved universal theory.  It is introduced as a disciplined grammar for stating what has been proved, what remains conditional, and what is blocked by an explicit missing object.

\section*{Purpose}

The manuscript has three purposes.  First, it fixes the vocabulary of refinement systems, transcript capacity, locality, and terminal obstruction.  Second, it collects reusable proof patterns.  Third, it records example domains without promoting those examples beyond their stated assumptions.

The present draft is therefore a controlled scaffold for later chapter-level expansion.

\section*{Boundary}

The textbook layer is exposition and organization unless a theorem is explicitly tied to a verified proof artifact.

\begin{remark}
A status label in this manuscript is not a substitute for a proof.  A closed certificate layer only closes the encoded certificate target named by that layer.
\end{remark}
