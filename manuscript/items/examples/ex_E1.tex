\begin{example}[SAT / Chronos]
\textbf{Dependencies: } T1, L2
\textbf{Status: } Illustrative

Consider random 3-SAT instances on $n$ variables near the satisfiability threshold.

Such instances satisfy $H(X)=\Theta(n)$ in assignment entropy.

Under bounded-width local refinement (e.g. resolution width $w=O(1)$),
each refinement step reduces entropy by at most $O(1)$.

By T1, total refinement depth satisfies
\[
T \ge \Omega(n).
\]

Thus linear sequential refinement depth is unavoidable under locality constraints.
\end{example}
