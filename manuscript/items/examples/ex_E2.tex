\begin{example}[Yang--Mills Spectral Gap Form]
\textbf{Dependencies: } D6, L4, T3
\textbf{Status: } Illustrative

Let $L$ denote the transfer Hamiltonian of a lattice Yang--Mills system,
and let $V$ denote the vacuum defect subspace.

If
\[
\lambda_1(L|_{V^\perp}) > 0,
\]
then all non-vacuum excitations satisfy uniform coercivity:
\[
\langle \psi, L \psi \rangle \ge \lambda_1 \|\psi\|^2.
\]

By T3, the mass gap problem reduces to verifying positivity
on the defect complement $V^\perp$.

Thus the Yang--Mills mass gap can be expressed as a spectral
terminal obstruction of the URF type.
\end{example}
